Chicken and Sausage Gumbo

Home

Description

This recipe is not quite a gumbo and more of a stew but we will call it gumbo since that is what inspired it. Regardless this is a hearty dish that can be tailored to your tastes and packs some spice and incredible protein content. Best served over rice and makes a great meal prep.

Equipment Needed

  • Dutch oven

Ingredients

  • 3/4 Cup butter
  • 3/4 Cup all purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil
  • 1 Onion chopped fine
  • 1 Green bell pepper chopped fine
  • 3 Celery ribs chopped fine
  • 5 Garlic cloves minced
  • 3tsp Paprika
  • 2tsp Cayenne pepper
  • 4 Cups chicken stock
  • 2lbs boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 8oz Andouille sausage cut into rounds
  • Scallions sliced thin

Steps

Make sure to add salt and taste throughout the cooking process.

  1. On the stove sweat the onions, peppers, and celery on medium high heat in some vegetable oil until the onions are turning translucent.
  2. At this point bring the heat down to medium and add in your garlic and seasonings and cook for another 5 minutes avoiding burning the garlic.
  3. Add in the stock and nestle in the chicken thighs so they are for the most part submerged. Bring to a simmer then set the heat to medium-low, cover it, and let it cook for 20 minutes or until the chicken thighs are done.
  4. While the chicken thighs cook make a dark cup of roux using the flour and butter being careful not to burn it.
  5. Remove the chicken thighs to a cutting board to let cool. Add the roux and andouille sausage while stirring to avoid clumps. Let this cook for 30-40min on low heat stirring occasionally.
  6. Shred the chicken and add it in with the scallions. Serve in a bowl over rice with some chopped fresh scallions on top.
